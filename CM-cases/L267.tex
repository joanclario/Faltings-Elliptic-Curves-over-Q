\section{Setting}
The isogeny graphs of type $L_2(67)$ are given by two isogenous elliptic curves:

\[ 
\begin{tikzcd}
E_1 \arrow[dash]{r}{67} & E_{67}\,.
\end{tikzcd}
\]

\noindent The modular curve $X_0(67)$ has genus $5$. Its rational points are: two rational cusps and one rational CM point $\tau=\frac{1}{2}+\frac{\sqrt{-67}}{2\cdot 67}\in\mathbb H$. Its corresponding $j$-invariants is:
$$
j(\tau)=-2^{15}\cdot 3^{3}\cdot 5^{3}\cdot 11^{3}.
$$
We have $j(67\tau)=j(\tau)$.

\

We can (and do) choose Weierstrass equations:
\vskip 0.5truecm
\begin{tblr}
{cells={mode=imath},hlines,vlines,measure=vbox,
colspec=clll}
E & \text{Minimal Weierstrass model} & j(E) &\text{label}\\
\hline
E_{1} & y^2 + y = x^3 - 7370x + 243528  & -2^{15}\cdot 3^{3}\cdot 5^{3}\cdot 11^{3} & \texttt{4489a1}\\
E_{67} & y^2 + y = x^3 - 33083930x - 73244287055 & -2^{15}\cdot 3^{3}\cdot 5^{3}\cdot 11^{3}  & \texttt{4489a2}\\
\end{tblr}
\vskip 0.5truecm

so that one has:
\vskip 0.5truecm

\begin{tblr}
{cells={mode=imath},hlines,vlines,measure=vbox,
colspec=cll}
%-------------------------------------------------
 E  & E_{1}& E_{67}\\
c_4(E) & 2^{5}\cdot 3\cdot 5\cdot 11\cdot 67 & 2^{5}\cdot 3\cdot 5\cdot 11\cdot 67^{3} \\
c_6(E) & -2^{3}\cdot 3^{3}\cdot 7\cdot 31\cdot 67^{2} & 2^{3}\cdot 3^{3}\cdot 7\cdot 31\cdot 67^{5}\\  
\Delta(E) &-67^{3}  &-67^{9} \\
\end{tblr}



\vskip 0.5truecm
\noindent and, with this choice, the isogeny graph is normalized. 

\

We have that the Faltings curve (circled)
in the graph is
\[
\begin{tblr}[mode=imath]{|c|c|}
\hline
 \circled[0.8]{$E_{1}$} \longrightarrow E_{67} \\
\hline
\end{tblr}
\]
Then any $\mathbb Q$-isogeny class of type $L_2(67)$ is isomorphic to $ 
\begin{tikzcd}
E_{1} \arrow[dash]{r}{67}  & E_{67} \,.
\end{tikzcd}
$
Note that $E_{1}$ and $E_{67}$ have complex multiplication by the ring of integers of $\mathbf{Q}(\sqrt{-67})$ and $E_{67}=E_{1}^{-67}$.
